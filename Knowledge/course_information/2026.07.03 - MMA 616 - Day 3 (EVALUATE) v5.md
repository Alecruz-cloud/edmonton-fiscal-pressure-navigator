NEW CONCEPT
The governance agent: a standing critic for your
own work
• What it is. An AI agent briefed to challenge your app rather than help build it. It uses the app the way a real user would, tests the claims the app
makes, and reports what fails, where, and why it matters.
• Separate context, adversarial brief. It starts fresh, with no memory of your build decisions and no stake in the outcome. Its instructions say
to find problems.
• A standing capability. You will run it this morning for a baseline and again this afternoon after your fixes. A critic you can re-run turns 'we
improved it' into evidence.
• The verdict is never delegated. The agent proposes findings. You decide which are real. Delegate Work, Not Accountability applies to
critique exactly as it applied to building.
Agents built your app. A different agent now attacks it, and you referee.
MMA 616 · Managing Intelligence 9

WHY A SEPARATE CRITIC
The checker cannot be the author
• Self-preferential bias is a tendency for a model to prefer its own results when asked to verify them (Shihipar & Bidasaria, 2026).
Ask the agent that drew the chart whether the chart misleads, and it leans toward telling you it is fine.
• Asking an author to grade its own work produces this bias in humans and models alike. The model is behaving normally.
• The remedy is structural. A separate agent with its own context, briefed only to challenge, is a harder critic than the author
reviewing itself.
• The same logic brings our guest this morning — James Freeman, who reviews pitches for a living — and it is why other groups
will read your app cold.
Do not ask the chart's author whether the chart is honest. Ask someone who did not draw it.

THE DISCIPLINE
Triage: every finding gets a verdict, from you
A critic's finding is a claim, not a fact. The same rule applies here as to every other agent output in this course.
1 Accept The finding is real and worth fixing. Put it on the fix list with a priority.
2 Reject The critic is wrong, or the behavior is intended. Write down why. A rejection without a reason is a dodge.
3 Defer Real, but not worth fixing before Saturday. Name it and park it. An honest deferral beats a silent one.
Saturday's viva will probe the triage record. 'Why did you reject this finding?' is a fair question.

MMA 616 · Managing Intelligence 25

NEW TOOL
The user story: one disciplined sentence
As a specific person, I want to do something in my working week, so that an outcome I actually care about happens. That is the
whole tool — one sentence, three slots.
Project managers invented it because builders kept shipping features nobody used. The sentence forces every feature to justify
itself through a person and a payoff, and it collapses when either one is vague.
Each slot forces something you must know. 'As a' demands a name — a job, a desk, a Tuesday, never 'a user.' 'I want to'
demands an action in their words, not your column names. 'So that' demands the payoff — it is where the value lives, and it is
the slot people skip.
Read your story backwards. No believable 'so that,' no story.

THE FIX
Most fixes are an adjustment, not a rebuild
• Once a finding has a home, the fix is usually small — a caveat surfaced where users kept missing it, a default view changed to
match how people actually work, a corrected filter, a sharpened spec line.
• Direct the change in plain language. Describe what the app should now do, the same way you directed the original build.
• Verification stays behavior-level. Test what the app now does and whether you can stand behind it — the course asks you to
audit behavior, and leaves the diff-reading to software engineers.
• Fix the top two or three accepted findings — the ones blocking story #1 first — and stop there. Depth per fix beats a shallow
sweep. Deferred findings, named honestly, belong in the assessment.

An agent you designed attacked your app, an investor questioned your pitch, five peer teams read it cold, and a week of
use left its notes.
Every finding crossed your judgment on the way in — accepted, rejected, or deferred, with reasons. The fixes went back
into the spec, and the same critic that found the problems confirmed the fixes.
That is the governance boundary doing its job, and it will still be useful when the tools have changed three times over.
Delegate work, not accountability. The verdict stays yours.
