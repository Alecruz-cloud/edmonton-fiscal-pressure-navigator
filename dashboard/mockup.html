Definition & formula verified

Total debt as a share of the regulated municipal debt limit. Amounts are nominal dollars; the ratio removes scale but not price effects.

Data quality & comparability

  • Nominal dollars. No inflation adjustment is applied; growth is not evidence of real cost change.
  • Population source rule. Per-capita measures use only the Alberta CSD population estimates (Edmonton = 4811061). The population field inside the financial workbooks is never used — it is stale (repeats 1,010,899 for 2023–2025).
  • Missing ≠ zero. Blank cells are recorded as missing, never converted to 0. The 2025 debt schedule alone holds 209 reported zeros and 4 blanks.
  • 2025 coverage. 306 of 332 returns received — every peer comparison on this page carries a provisional label.

Purpose and period

This brief identifies the financial pressure areas that deserve deeper management review before the coming budget cycle, using Edmonton's 2018–2025 financial returns and Alberta CSD population estimates. 2025 peer coverage is provisional (306 of 332 returns).

Top signals for review

  1. Debt utilization — Watch. 59.27% of the regulated limit in 2025; above the 2018–19 range, down from the 2022 peak of 64.11%. status illustrative

Evidence

  • Every figure above traces to workbook › schedule › variable code › year; traces are shown in the drill-down view.
  • Edmonton's own 2018–2025 history is the primary benchmark for every test.
  • Calgary and Alberta-city context is supporting evidence only and is labelled provisional for 2025.

Limitations

  • Dollar amounts are nominal; no inflation adjustment has been approved or applied.
  • Descriptive trends only — no causal claims, no forecasts.
  • Signal thresholds are analytical heuristics documented in governance › pressure_rules.md, not City policy.

Follow-up questions for management

  • Illustrative: what share of remaining debt headroom is already committed in the approved capital plan?
  • Illustrative: which functions drive per-capita expense movement, and are they demand-driven?
This brief does not make policy recommendations. It flags areas for further analysis and management attention. It does not assess Edmonton as financially healthy or unhealthy, and no single figure in it should be read as such an assessment.
